Early-life and Education

Abdul Kalam's Early-life and Education

Dr. Kalam Aeronautical engineering Dr. Kalam was born into a modest family and grew up in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu. His father was a boat owner and his mother was a homemaker. Despite coming from a humble background, Dr. Kalam was an excellent student and earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Madras Institute of Technology in 1958.

Contributions to Science and Technology

Abdul Kalam's Contributions to Science and Technology

(DRDO) chief project coordinatorDr. Kalam joined India's Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1960 and played a pivotal role in developing India's ballistic missile program. He was the chief project coordinator for the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III. Dr. Kalam was also the chief scientific adviser to the Indian government and played a crucial role in the successful nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998.

Presidency and Public Service

Abdul Kalam's Presidency and Public Service

President of India Dr. Kalam was elected as the President of India in 2002 and served in this role until 2007. As President, he traveled extensively throughout the country, meeting with students, teachers, and other citizens to discuss his vision for India's future. Dr. Kalam was a strong advocate for education, and he launched the PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas) initiative to improve the quality of life in rural India.

Teaching and Writing

Abdul Kalam's Teachings and Writings

inspirational booksIn addition to his work in science and public service, Dr. Kalam was also a highly respected teacher and author. He wrote many inspirational books, including Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and My Journey: Transforming Dreams into Actions. He was a visiting professor at many universities around the world and inspired countless students with his lectures and speeches.

Legacy and Honors

Abdul Kalam's Legacy and Honors

Bharat ratna Padma bhushan Padma vibhushanDr. Kalam's contributions to science, education, and public service have left an indelible mark on India and the world. In recognition of his many achievements, Dr. Kalam was awarded numerous honors and awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan. After his passing, the government of India declared his birthday, October 15th, as "World Student Day" to honor his contributions to education.
